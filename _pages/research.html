People who deactivated Facebook for the six weeks before the election reported a 0.060 standard deviation improvement in an index of happiness, depression, and anxiety, relative to controls who deactivated for just the first of those six weeks. People who deactivated Instagram for those six weeks reported a 0.041 standard deviation improvement relative to controls. Exploratory analysis suggests the Facebook effect is driven by people over 35, while the Instagram effect is driven by women under 25.* [hallcott]: https://allcott.stanford.edu/ [mgentzkow]: https://www.matthewgentzkow.com/ [bwitten]: https://benjaminhwittenbrink.github.io/
